FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Everything You Need to Know

What is the Golden Defender project?

+
The Golden Defender is a complete restoration of a classic 1981 Defender arcade cabinet. This project documents every step of bringing this vintage machine back to its original glory, from initial discovery through final testing and gameplay.

How long did the restoration take?

+
The complete restoration took approximately 100+ hours over several months. This included disassembly, cleaning, part replacement, painting, reassembly, and extensive testing.

What were the biggest challenges?

+
Finding authentic replacement parts was the biggest challenge, especially for the monitor and circuit boards. Many components are no longer manufactured, requiring creative solutions and sourcing from specialty suppliers.

Is the cabinet playable now?

+
Yes! After complete restoration and testing, the cabinet is fully functional and plays just like it did in 1981. All original controls, display, and sound systems are working perfectly.

Can I see it in person?

+
The cabinet is part of a private collection. Please use the contact form if you're interested in arranging a viewing or have questions about the restoration process.

Do you restore other arcade machines?

+
This website documents the Golden Defender restoration. For inquiries about other projects or restoration services, please reach out through the contact page.

Where can I find parts for my own restoration?

+
Check the "Golden Parts" page for a detailed list of components and suppliers used in this restoration. Many specialty arcade parts vendors exist online, and community forums can be invaluable resources.

Can I use your photos or documentation?

+
For personal reference and education, feel free to use the information. For any commercial or public use, please contact us first for permission.

What advice do you have for beginners?

+
Start with thorough documentation and photos before disassembly. Take your time, join arcade restoration communities, and don't be afraid to ask for help. Every restoration teaches you something new!
